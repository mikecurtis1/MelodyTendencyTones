Tendency Tones Melody

Tendency Tones Melody is javascript application to generate a random melody which implements melodic tendency tone rules as given by Kent Kennan in his book “Counterpoint”.

  • Scale degree ^7 tends to resolve to ^1
  • ^4 tends to resolve to ^3
  • ^6 tends to resolve to ^5
  • To a lesser extent ^2 tends to resolve to ^1
  • Any tendency tone - ^7, ^4, ^6, or ^2 - may move to another tendency tone, resolution follows the last tendency tone of series of tendency tones

The follow exceptions are possible for moving contrary to a tendency tone's normal resolution.

  • ^7 when preceeded by ^1 may continue to ^6 as (^1 ^7 ^6)
  • ^4 when preceeded by ^3 may continue to ^5 as (^3 ^4 ^5)
  • ^6 when preceeded by ^5 may continue to ^7 as (^5 ^6 ^7)
  • ^2 when preceeded by ^1 may continue to ^3 as (^1 ^2 ^3)

I add to Keenan's tendency tone rule these simple points:

  • Limit the range of the melody to a perfect eleventh (P11), specifically B3 to E5
  • Each melody tone should not repeat either of the two previous tones
  • No more than two consecutive tendency tones
  • Conclude the melody line with either a half cadence or authentic cadence

Harmonically some scale degree have one clear chord association while other can be associated with multiple chords and therefore are harmonically ambiguous.

  • Scale degrees ^1 and ^3 are clearly assoicated with I while scale degree ^7 is clearly associated with V.
  • Scale degree ^6 is usually assoicated with IV, however in a line like ^7 ^6 ^5 degree ^6 seems to be part of a retrogression (V IV V.) In such cases ^6 can be harmonized as V/V.
  • Scale degree ^5 can be harmonized with either I or V.
  • Scale degrees ^2 and ^4 can be harmonized with either IV or V. Their tendency tone resolution alone does not determine the harmonization (ex. ^4 to ^3 can be either IV I or V7 I.)
  • In the case of ^4 the melodic direction has bearing. Degree ^4 ascending to ^5 with harmonization V7 I would be an irregular resolution.
  • A possibility for ^4 ascending to ^5 is a voice exchange as V7 to another voicing of V7.
  • In any event if a harmonization presents a retrogression (ex. ^7 to ^2 harmonized with V IV) an alternative is to harmonize the second chord as another V or possibly V/V.

The final melody is presented in unmetered whole notes. It could be realized in various meters, rhythms, and with ornamentation. One consideration when choosing rhythms is the melodic tendency to leap to weak beats. This works well with leaps that are “filled” by a step. Leaps among tonic chord tones, or other clear chord groups, or leaps between tendency tones could be played with even rhythm values.
